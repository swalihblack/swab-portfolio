# Redesigning a Checkout Flow to Reduce Cart Abandonment

**Role:** Lead Product Designer  
**Duration:** 4 weeks  
**Tools:** Figma, Hotjar, Maze

![Cover image for the checkout redesign case study](cover-checkout.jpg)

## The Problem

Glow Skincare, a direct-to-consumer beauty brand, was facing a **60% cart abandonment rate**. Users added items to their cart but dropped off right before completing the purchase. Session recordings showed confusion around shipping costs and a lack of progress indicators.

## Research & Discovery

I analyzed three weeks of Hotjar recordings and surveyed 50 recent customers. Key insights:

- Users expected a progress indicator but didn’t see one.
- Shipping costs appeared only after entering personal details.
- Mobile users struggled with small input fields.

![Hotjar heatmap showing high drop-off at the shipping step](heatmap-checkout.png)

## Design Iterations

I started with low‑fidelity wireframes to test a new information architecture. After three rounds of usability testing with Maze, I landed on a **one‑column, step‑by‑step checkout** with a persistent order summary.

![Low-fidelity wireframes of the new checkout flow](wireframes-checkout.png)

## Visual Design & Implementation

The final design introduced:

- A sticky “Order Summary” sidebar on desktop.
- Real-time shipping cost estimator.
- Trust badges near the payment button.

I collaborated with a frontend developer to implement the design, ensuring accessibility and responsive behavior.

## Results

After launch:

- **Cart abandonment dropped by 32%** in the first month.
- Support tickets related to “where is my order” decreased by 45%.
- CSAT for checkout improved from 3.2 to 4.6.

## What I Learned

This project reinforced the value of testing early with real users. If I were to do it again, I’d A/B test a simplified guest checkout to further reduce friction.
